## Ingredients

QTY | Ing
---|---
3 tbsp | ghee
1 | large onion, diced
4 | cloves garlic, rough chopped
2 tbsp | ginger, finely chopped
½ tsp | fennel seeds
1 tsp | black mustard seeds
2 tsp | cumin seeds
2 tsp | garam masala
400g | baby spinach (fresh or frozen)
20 | fresh mint leaves
½ cup | plain yogurt (or vegan yogurt)
3–4  cups | cooked black lentils or French green lentils
1 tsp | salt

## Method
[[Lentils]]
### Dahl
1.  **Make the Spinach Sauce:** Sautee the onion in ghee, in a large pan, over medium heat for 3-4 minutes
2. Add garlic, ginger and chilies. Saute until fragrant and golden. 
3. Add the fennel seeds, mustard seeds, cumin seeds and garam masala and stir two minutes. 
4. Lower heat to low. Add the fresh spinach, fresh mint, fenugreek and 2 tablespoons water. Cover pan, 2-3 minutes, letting spinach wilt. Give a few stirs. Don’t overcook!
5.  **Blend Spinach Sauce:** Place the wilted spinach mixture in a blender and add the 3/4 cups of water. Pulse and few times (hold the lid down tight). If you want a smooth sauce, blend until smooth. 
6. Pour it back into the pan, set on low heat. Don’t overheat or you will lose the pretty color.
7.  **Combine:** Stir in the yogurt, lentils and salt. You want this slightly more salty because you are serving over rice which will mellow it out a lot. For more heat add a **pinch of cayenne or chili flakes.**  If you want a little acidity a tiny squeeze of lemon is nice.
8.  **Serve** with the basmati rice and naan bread

*Emerald Dal will keep up to 4 days in the fridge.*
